How We Can Help To Save The Sea Turtles

How We Can Prevent

⊛The dangers to sea turtles and other marine species-fishery effects, coastal development, poaching, pollution, and global warming are caused by human actions that we can all influence since we live in a linked world. Here are some of our recommendations for leading a more
ocean and sea turtle-friendly lifestyle.


1].Minimize Fishery Impacts

Sea turtles and other marine animals may benefit or suffer as a result of the seafood you choose to eat. Everyone benefits from wise seafood selections.



SELECT YOUR SEAFOOD CAREFULLY.

When choosing seafood for yourself or guests, whether at home or out and about, online resources like Seafood Watch (also accessible as a mobile application) and certification programs like the Marine Stewardship Council can be of great assistance. Use your purchasing power to advance this important sector by adhering to sustainable seafood rules.



DISSEMBLE THE WORD

Inform your loved ones about the consequences of their seafood selections. Work together with neighborhood businesses, schools, and community organizations to spread the word about the benefits of eating locally fished seafood. Discuss the significance of choosing sustainable seafood with the personnel at your local eateries, the managers of your local grocery stores, and other community members.



VOTE FOR THE OCEAN

Keep up with current fisheries concerns and let your government know that you favor greater fishing sector regulation that safeguards the marine environment. Support candidates for office who are dedicated to ocean stewardship.




2].Reduce Pollution

Sea turtles are impacted by pollution in a variety of ways, both directly and indirectly. Make a list of the products you already use and consider what you can replace with more ecologically friendly options and what you can do without.



REDUCE, REUSE, RECYCLE

The best ways to reduce pollution are still to reduce, reuse, and recycle. The more efficiently you use what you already own and the less single-use products you consume, the better. Change from disposable plastic bottles to reusable metal or plastic bottles and from disposable plastic bags to reusable cloth bags.



GET INVOLVED IN OR HOST A CLEANUP

Your garbage can harm sea turtles and other marine life even if you don't live close to the ocean since it can be carried there by rivers or the wind. Keep it from going that far! Join the yearly International Coastal Cleanup or organize a neighborhood cleanup on your own to gather rubbish off the shoreline of a nearby river, park, or road.




3].Protect Coastlines

Sea turtles and other creatures share coastlines with us, making them precious places. We can contribute to preserving these areas' vitality and health for future generations by choosing better ways to use them.



ADVOCATE FOR TURLE-FRIENDLY TOURISM

Choose to stay at a hotel or resort that is making an effort to protect its surroundings while you are organizing your next beach trip. Verify that the location has taken the required precautions to safeguard the safety of nesting females, nests, and hatchlings before visiting a sea turtle nesting habitat. Do they turn off the lights at night? teach visitors how to behave when watching? Support or collaborate with a program for beach monitoring? If not, ask them to, and if they do, tell them how much you value it.



LEARN THE BEACH'S RULES

Follow these simple guidelines to lessen your influence on sea turtles, whether you live near one or are simply visiting:


  • While walking on the beach at night, make minimal use of your flashlight.
  • If you encounter a sea turtle, do not touch, shine a light on, or take flash photographs of it; remain quiet, watch from a distance, and enjoy the moment.
  • Keep all rubbish contained and safe while at the beach, and take it with you when you leave along with your other possessions. After having fun in the sand, patch up any holes to make the transition from surf to sand as easy for sea turtles as possible.
  • To stop your pets from hurting eggs and hatchlings, keep them on a leash at all times.
  • Drive carefully when on the sand. Even in regions where driving is allowed, driving on the sand can damage incubating eggs and imprison hatchlings as they attempt to swim to the sea.



4].Decrease Direct Take

Around the world, sea turtles are killed for food and for profit, both officially and illegally. The unlawful international trade of turtle products poses a serious threat to sea turtles in general and hawksbills in particular. The consumer market for sea turtle items can be eliminated with the aid of all of us.



DO NOT EAT OR BUY SEA TURTLE PRODUCTS.

Many people are startled to hear that jewelry, souvenirs, and other products are still frequently made from tortoiseshell (from the hawksbill turtle) and marketed to visitors, particularly in the Caribbean, Latin America, and Asia. Make no mistake, tortoiseshell cannot be obtained from sustainable sources, so these products must always be avoided. When traveling, make cautious to avoid anything made of tortoiseshell by learning how to identify it. Please report any tortoiseshell items you find.






5].Fight Climate Change

Despite the fact that we may not yet fully comprehend the wide-ranging implications of climate change, we do so enough to recognize that doing something about it is one of the most urgent concerns of our time. Rising sea levels and temperatures have already had an impact on sea turtle ecosystems like nesting beaches and coral reefs, but we can help prevent additional harm by taking action to slow down climate change right away.


To Begin:-

  • Turn Off - Be sure to switch off the lights before leaving a room.
  • Plugging in appliances that are not in use might still drain energy even when they are switched off. Use power strips to make it simpler to turn off several devices at once.
  • Make a Switch - Assess your commute to see whether you can cycle, walk, ride in a carpool, or use public transportation.
