SEA TURTLE HATCHERIES

Turtle Hatcheries In Sri Lanka



★Turtle hatcheries are facilities specifically designed to protect and aid in the conservation of sea turtles. These organizations or establishments focus on ensuring the survival of sea turtle populations by monitoring nesting sites, collecting and incubating turtle eggs, and releasing hatchlings back into the ocean.


★The primary goals of turtle hatcheries are to safeguard sea turtle nests from natural threats and human activities, promote successful hatching rates, and provide a safe environment for the vulnerable hatchlings until they are ready to be released. These hatcheries play a crucial role in turtle conservation efforts, especially in areas where sea turtles face significant challenges due to habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and poaching.



★Sri Lanka is known for its sea turtle conservation efforts, and there are several turtle hatcheries located throughout the country. These hatcheries play a vital role in protecting and conserving sea turtle populations, as Sri Lanka is home to five species of sea turtles: the Green turtle, Loggerhead turtle, Hawksbill turtle, Olive Ridley turtle, and Leatherback turtle. Here are some well known turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka:

  • Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery
  • Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery
  • Bentota Turtle Hatchery
